Privacy Policy

Effective June 9, 2026

Amprov is designed to help nearby people hear a live speaker. This policy explains how the Amprov MVP handles information.

The short version

Amprov uses your microphone and local network to provide a live audio session. The MVP is designed to transmit audio to devices in your room, not to record it or store it on Amprov servers. The marketing website does not use analytics, advertising cookies, or contact forms.

Information the app uses

When you use Amprov, the app may use or display:

  • Microphone audio: If you host a room, Amprov needs microphone access to transmit your live voice to joined devices.
  • Local network access: Amprov uses the local network to discover rooms and connect nearby devices.
  • Room details: Room names and temporary passcodes help people identify and join a session.
  • Device and session details: The app may display device names, connection status, battery level, and signal quality during a live session.

How information is used

Information is used only to provide and operate the live room experience, including discovering rooms, connecting participants, transmitting live audio, and showing session status.

Audio and retention

The Amprov MVP is intended for live audio transmission. It does not intentionally record or retain microphone audio after a live session ends. Room passcodes and live session details are temporary and intended to last only as long as needed to operate the session.

Sharing

Amprov does not sell personal information. Live audio and session details are shared with devices that join the same Amprov room. People should only host or join rooms they trust, and hosts should share passcodes carefully.

This website

This static marketing website is hosted by GitHub Pages. Amprov does not add analytics, advertising trackers, or cookies to it. GitHub may process technical information when serving the site under its own privacy practices.

Children's privacy

Amprov is not directed to children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13.

Security

We take reasonable steps to design Amprov with privacy and security in mind. No method of electronic transmission is completely secure, so only use Amprov on networks and with participants you trust.

Changes to this policy

As the MVP evolves, Amprov's data practices may change. We will update this page and its effective date when they do.

Contact

Questions or concerns can be submitted through the Amprov GitHub repository.
