# Amprov Brand Style Guide

## Brand Overview

Amprov is a simple mobile app that turns a group of smartphones into an instant PA system.

A host phone acts as a microphone. Nearby phones become speakers.

The goal is not professional audio.

The goal is helping groups hear a speaker clearly without requiring special equipment.

### Tagline

Everyone can hear now.

This tagline should influence every design decision.

The product is about inclusion, participation, accessibility, and community.

The emotional outcome is not "better audio."

The emotional outcome is:

> Everyone feels included because everyone can hear.

---

# Target Audience

Primary users are community organizers rather than technology enthusiasts.

Examples include:

- Sports parents organizing team banquets
- Coaches and volunteer coordinators
- Church leaders
- Nonprofit board members
- School PTO/PTA volunteers
- Retreat organizers
- Community event hosts

Typical age range:

- Millennials
- Younger Gen X

Technology comfort:

- Comfortable using smartphones
- Understand QR codes
- Can join WiFi networks
- Not interested in technical complexity

They value:

- Simplicity
- Reliability
- Practical solutions
- Community

---

# Brand Personality

## Friendly

The product should feel approachable and welcoming.

Avoid aggressive or highly technical aesthetics.

## Trustworthy

People may be relying on the app moments before an event begins.

The brand should inspire confidence.

## Helpful

The app solves a problem.

It should feel like a useful tool rather than a gadget.

## Community-Oriented

The brand should emphasize people gathering together.

Think:

- Banquets
- Church picnics
- Team celebrations
- Volunteer events
- Outdoor gatherings

# Logo Direction

## Preferred Concept

The logo combines:

- A microphone
- A capital letter A

The current preferred direction is a microphone silhouette where the interior negative space forms a capital A.

The A should be immediately recognizable once noticed.

The microphone should remain recognizable even at small app icon sizes.

## Design Characteristics

- Rounded corners
- Soft geometry
- Friendly proportions
- Minimal detail
- Strong silhouette

Avoid:

- Sharp corners
- Aggressive angles
- Excessive realism
- Vintage microphone aesthetics

---

# App Icon Direction

Preferred treatment:

- Rounded-square iOS style icon
- Green background
- Glass or "liquid glass" microphone mark
- White or translucent logo treatment
- Broadcast waves on both sides

The icon should communicate:

1. Microphone
2. Communication
3. Amplification

without requiring text.

---

# Visual Language

## Use

- Rounded shapes
- Generous whitespace
- Soft shadows
- Glass effects where appropriate
- Simple illustrations
- Human-centered photography

## Avoid

- Heavy skeuomorphism
- Dark mode-first branding
- Overly technical diagrams
- Dense interfaces
- Complex iconography

# Messaging Principles

Lead with outcomes.

Do not lead with technology.

Prefer:

"Everyone can hear now."

"Turn phones into an instant speaker system."

"No special equipment required."

"Perfect for outdoor events."

"Ready in under a minute."

Avoid:

"Low-latency distributed audio."

"Wireless network synchronization."

"Peer-to-peer audio streaming."

"Advanced DSP technology."

The user cares about hearing the speaker, not how it works.

---

# Brand Test

A successful Amprov design should make a sports parent say:

> "Oh, that's the app we should use for the banquet."

before they think:

> "Interesting audio technology."
